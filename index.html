End Of Life

A Third Person Shooter game developed using C++ and Unreal Engine, as a Master's degree thesis at U-Tad university. I worked as the lead AI programmer and producer.

Worked on:

  • C++
  • Unreal Engine
  • AI
  • Performance Improvement
  • UI


End Of Life tells the story of Nashla, a demi goddess that needs to defeat her enemies and find her way in fragmented and moving world shaped similarly to a Rubik's Cube. With her weapons, her lethal needles, and her powers, such as the abilities to slow time or rotate the world, she will try to restore her magic to its former glory.

Due to the frequent gravity changes in the game, we had to design and implement the AI movement ourselves, this involved creating a Navigation Grid adapted to the game's environment, as well as programming the NPC's movement and path finding.

As lead AI programmer, I created and implemented the Path Following, Behaviour Trees, EQS (Environment Query System), worked on AI performance improvement and completed any other NPC behaviour related task. Additionally, I played a significant role on UI implementation.

Finally, as one of two producers, I organized a 19 people team, focusing on task management, decision making and conflict resolution.

Social MatchUp

An online Oculus VR game for kids with neurodivergent disorders during Covid, developed with C# and Unity as a University project at Politecnico di Milano.

To win this mini-game, the two kids were faced with multiple emojis, each representing a different emotion, and had to communicate to select the same one at the same time. The goal was to help them associate feelings with face expressions and learn communication skills. Additionally, a third player could join the game as the therapist or adult, to help the kids whenever needed without having to be in the same room.

Tools:

  • C#
  • Unity
  • Online
  • Photon Engine
  • Oculus VR
